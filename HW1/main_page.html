What is HCI?

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is the study of how people use computers and digital systems. The goal is to design interfaces that are simple, clear, and helpful.

Why does this matter?

Good HCI helps users finish tasks faster and with fewer mistakes. Bad design can confuse users, cause frustration, and lead to errors.

A famous example is the Three Mile Island Nuclear disaster, which was shown to, in part, be caused by poor human-machine interface design.

What is the importance of HCI in relation to computer science?

  • Research in this field directly helps developers design easier-to-use software
  • Combines knowledge from a variety of different fields from both the machine side (OS, computer graphics) and the human side (linguistics, psychology)
  • Most of the research within this field takes a lot of inspiration in the way software and interfaces are developed
  • Supports accessibility and inclusive design to improve the way in which we are able to use computers
  • Much of the current research focuses on how things like AI can be integrated into applications to improve the way in which users use a piece of software
