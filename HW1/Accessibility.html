What is accessibility?

Accessibility in computing means designing technology so that it can be used by as many people as possible, including individuals with disabilities. This includes people with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive impairments.

Why accessibility matters

Accessible design helps remove barriers that prevent users from interacting with software. It improves usability for everyone and ensures that digital systems are inclusive and fair.

Accessibility in computer science

Accessibility is an important part of human-computer interaction and software development. Developers and designers must consider accessibility when creating websites, applications, and hardware interfaces.

Common accessibility considerations

  • Providing text alternatives for images and media
  • Ensuring sufficient color contrast for readability
  • Supporting keyboard navigation
  • Designing clear layouts and readable text
  • Making interfaces compatible with assistive technologies

By incorporating accessibility principles early in the design process, developers can create systems that are easier to use and more inclusive for all users.
