What is User Experience (UX)?

User experience focuses on how people feel when using a product or system. UX design aims to make interactions simple, efficient, and satisfying by considering user needs, goals, and context.

Questions UX designers ask

  • Who are the users?
  • What tasks are they trying to complete?
  • How easily can they reach their goals?
  • What problems or frustrations might occur?

Core UX Principles

Understand the user

UX design begins by understanding users through research such as interviews, observation, and basic usability testing.

Keep tasks simple

Interfaces should reduce confusion by breaking tasks into clear steps and avoiding unnecessary information.

Provide feedback

Systems should respond to user actions with clear feedback such as confirmations, error messages, or visual changes.

Design for accessibility

UX design supports users with different abilities by providing readable text, clear navigation, and accessible controls.

Common UX methods

  • User research
  • Wireframes
  • Prototypes
  • Usability testing
